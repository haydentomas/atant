St. Anthony’s Sheltered Housing Complex was built in 1973 for Poles who wished to spend the later period of their lives in a typical polish surrounding. The Home provides a Polish atmosphere and is situated near a Polish Restaurant and the Polish Church. 

Our complex is a part of PCM Housing Association Ltd., the organisation that was funded in 1965 to support polish people living in Great Britain. We are registered under The Industrial & Provident Societies Act 1965 number 17061 R.

Our building is a sheltered complex where each resident occupies their own flat. Here you can find more detailed information about us, everyday life in our Home and about help we provide.

What is important to us:

Help

At St.Anthony’s, staff are available to support tenants throughout the day and at night time. We will give you advice and support on any issues or problems you may have.

Safety

Five members of staff work on shifts maintaining the safety and security of the residents and the building 24 hours a day 7 days a week.

Tradition

We care about maintaining Polish tradition through various meetings during the year.
